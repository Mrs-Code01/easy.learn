THE EVOLUTION OF THE NIGERIA STATE (BERLIN TREATY)

The Berlin treaty was as a result of the rivalry between Great Britain and France. This treaty meeting was by Bismarck to intervene and bring the rivalry to an end. He called the European powers for a meeting in the Berlin conference of 1884/1885. In subsequent meetings, Great Britain, France, Germany, Portugal and Leopold II of Belgium negotiated their claims to African territories, which were then formalized and mapped.

FEATURES / CHARACTERISTICS OF THE TREATY 1884 / 1885

1. FRIENDLY PREFACE: This feature of the Berlin treaty was to enable the British who had no previous history of protracted / long relations to maintain peaceful co-existence. Secondly, the above treaty was made because the British and Itsekiri chiefs were desirous of maintaining and strengthening the relation of peace and friendship which had so long existed between them.

2. DISPUTE ARTICLE: This treaty allowed the subjects and citizens of all countries to freely carry on trade in every part of the territories of kings and chiefs and have houses and factories therein.

3. PROTECTION ARTICLES: The above treaty contained that all ministers of the Christian religion should be permitted to reside and exercise their calling within the territories of Kings and Chiefs who thereby guaranteed to them full protection. Secondly, all forms of religious worship and religious ordinances might be exercised within the territories and no hindrance should be offered.

NIGERIA’S COLONIAL GOVERNORS GENERAL

1. SIR FREDERICK LORD LUGARD (1919 - 1925):

He was born on January 22, 1858. He was an administrator who played major part in Britain’s colonial history.

ACHIEVEMENTS OF LORD LUGGARD

1. He amalgamated the Northern and Southern protectorate on January 1, 1994.
2. He suppressed the Fulani’s revolt in Nigeria
3. Through him, his wife Lady Flora Shaw named the country Nigeria.

4. He enforced the abolition of slavery and slave trade in Nigeria.
5. He introduced indirect rule in Nigeria, and inaugurated the Nigerian council 1914.



SHORTCOMINGS OF HIS ADMINISTRATION

1. He permitted slavery within traditional elite’s family.

2. Autocratic administration.
3. He blocked educated and qualified Africans from playing active role in the development of the country.



2. SIR HUGH CLIFFORD (1919 - 1925):

He was born on the 5th of March, 1866.

His achievements include:
a. Introduction of first formal constitution.
b. Introduction of elective principle.
c. Encouraged the formation of political parties.
